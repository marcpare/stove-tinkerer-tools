The Water Boiling Test provides a simple metric to help you compare the performance of stoves.

The calculations on this page are a "finger in the wind" to guide testing, a first glimpse into the depth of engineering that goes into improved cook stove design.

Energy in Wood (HV): 31,000 kJ/kg

Effective calorific value (EHV) is calculated by subtracting the amount of energy required to vaporize the 15% moisture content.

EHV = LHV * (1-MC)-(MC*((HHV-T)*4.2+2260))

where

EHV Effective Heating Value kJ/kg
LHV Lower Heating Value kJ/kg
MC Fuel Moisture Content %
T Air Temperature C
4.2 Heat Vaporization of Water C
2260 Enthalpy Water kcal/kg-C
